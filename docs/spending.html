Inside the Schools Budget

School Budget Components (FY20–FY26)

Teacher salaries are the largest component at $57.7M (FY25), growing 40% since FY20. Paraprofessional salaries nearly doubled (+95%) driven by special education caseloads. Special education overall ($27.6M) grows 8–12% annually — the fastest category — and is legally mandated regardless of budget constraints.
Source: Arlington School Committee Budget Documents FY20–FY27

Pensions & Retiree Benefits

Pension Costs: Mandated Path to FY2040

Pensions grow 5.5–6% annually under a state-mandated schedule toward full funding by FY2040. Arlington’s system grew from 774 active employees + 615 retirees (FY2016) to ~856 active + ~700 retirees (FY2025). Cost per member rose from $6,548 to $9,644 (+47%) while total members grew 12%. The cost increase is driven primarily by higher per-capita obligations (closing the unfunded liability), not just more people.
Source: Arlington Retirement System / PERAC Actuarial Schedule

OPEB: Liability, Assets & Funded %

OPEB is the one genuine success story — funded percentage improved from 3.5% to 16.9% through disciplined annual contributions. But $147M in unfunded liability remains.
Source: Arlington OPEB Actuarial Valuations
