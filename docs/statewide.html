State Funding & Mandates

State Reimbursement for School Construction

Source: MSBA Reimbursement Rates
Before the 2004 MSBA reform (which created the Massachusetts School Building Authority), the state reimbursed ~63% of school construction costs under the School Building Assistance (SBA) program. In the reform era (2004–2015), the new MSBA restructured the process so the state selects and prioritizes projects; the 50% figure represents the average reimbursement rate across all accepted projects statewide, but wealthier suburbs receive less. For Arlington’s AHS rebuild (approved 2019), the effective rate was only 29% — shifting $204M to local property taxpayers.

Health Insurance (GIC)

What is the GIC? The Group Insurance Commission is a state agency that arranges and administers health insurance for state and participating municipal employees and retirees. Arlington joined the GIC in FY2013. Rates are set annually by the Commission — municipalities have no say in the rate decisions.

GIC Health Insurance Rate Changes

Source: GIC Annual Reports
Arlington has no control over GIC rates. The 12.7% FY2026 spike alone added $3M+ to the budget. Three consecutive years of double-digit increases (FY2025–FY2027) have erased the savings from joining the GIC.

Special Education: Federal Mandates, Local Costs

Source: MA DESE / Arlington Budget Reports
  • Pensions: State law mandates municipalities fully fund pension liabilities by FY2040. Arlington's annual pension cost grows 5.5-6% — non-negotiable.
  • Special Education: Federal law (IDEA) requires districts to provide services for every student with an IEP regardless of cost. Arlington's SpEd budget grows 6.5-7% annually — the fastest-growing category.

The Structural Gap

The Prop 2½ Structural Gap

Source: Arlington Financial Plans / Prop 2½ Model
With costs growing 4-6% annually and revenue capped at ~3.3% (2.5% + 0.8% new growth), the gap compounds every year. After 20 years, cumulative excess cost exceeds $70M annually.
