The Sawtooth Pattern

What is the Override Stabilization Fund? When Arlington passes a Prop 2½ override, the additional tax revenue exceeds immediate needs in the first few years. The surplus is deposited into the Override Stabilization Fund, which is then drawn down in later years as costs grow beyond what the levy can support. When the fund runs out, a new override is needed.

The fund follows a predictable pattern: override passes → fund builds → costs outpace revenue → fund drains → new override needed. Each cycle is shorter than the last — shrinking from 6 years to 3 years as cost growth compounds faster on a larger budget base. In 2003, a $4M override attempt failed by 687 votes — the only failed override in Arlington’s modern history.
