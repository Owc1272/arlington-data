Peer Comparisons: Why Arlington's Revenue Lags

New Growth Rate — 5-Year Average

Arlington's new growth rate (0.80%) is last among all 13 peer communities. The peer average is 1.89%. This alone costs Arlington ~$1.8M/year in forgone revenue.
Source: MA DOR Municipal Data

Commercial/Industrial Share of Tax Base

Arlington's commercial base (5.34%) is one of the smallest among peers. Brookline (27.5%) and Watertown (23%) benefit from commercial corridors that Arlington lacks. Residential taxpayers bear 95% of the burden.
Source: MA DOR Municipal Data

State Aid to Arlington

Arlington's share of total state aid fell ~34% from 1989 to 2025. Chapter 70 school aid grew just 0.97% in FY2025 — less than half the rate of school cost growth.
Source: Arlington Town Manager Financial Plans

Municipal Revenue Growth Factor

The revenue growth factor (levy limit growth + new growth) determines how fast a town's revenue can grow. Arlington's 3.24 is 12th of 13 peers (avg: 4.50).
Source: MA DOR Municipal Data
